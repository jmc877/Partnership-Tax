Partnership Tax: Overview

Introduction and Learning Outcomes

Welcome to Partnership Tax! The goal of this course is to acquaint you with the fundamental principles of partnership taxation.

The class focuses on understanding the basic U.S. tax regime applicable to domestic partnerships. In particular, we will cover and by the end of the course you should have a basic understanding of the following topics:

  • Existence of a partnership or other business arrangement
  • Consequences of contributions of property to a partnership
  • Understanding partnership operations, including the flexibility in allocating income, gains, and losses among partners
  • Special rules applicable to contributions of property with BIG and BIL
  • The relationship between liabilities and outside basis
  • Partner-partnership transactions
  • Sales of partnership interests
  • Consequences of distributions
  • Disguised sales


The knowledge gained in this class is extremely valuable in related corporate law classes, such as M&A, corporate reorganizations, securities regulation, introduction to the deal, venture capital, and private equity.

Problem Sets

You cannot learn the material without practice. You should try to do as many of the problems in the book as you can. Free free to skip those that involve a lot of algebra. I will also assign homeworks (based on prior classes, between 7-10) that will be graded. The homeworks must be typed.You will be assigned numbers so that the homeworks will be graded anonymously. You can work in groups of up to 3. If you work with other people, please submit only 1 homework. The due date for each homework will be announced when it is posted, and you will generally have 1 week to complete it. Late homeworks will not be graded. In computing your homework grade, I will drop the lowest score. The homeworks will account for roughly 15% of your grade.
